## VALIDATION CRITERIA

Evaluate the analysis on these dimensions:

1. **IOC Accuracy** — Are IOC types correctly classified? Are reported attacker IPs actually public?
   Are hash lengths correct for stated algorithm? Are domains plausible threat actor infrastructure?

2. **ATT&CK Mapping** — Do all technique IDs exist and follow T[0-9]{{4}}(.0[0-9]{{2}})? format?
   Are techniques assigned to the correct tactics? Are there obvious missing techniques for the campaign type?

3. **Kill Chain Logic** — Is the attack sequence logically ordered?
   Does each stage build on the previous? Are there violations (exfil before collection, etc.)?

4. **Attribution Confidence** — Is the stated confidence_level appropriate given the evidence?
   Is threat_actor claimed with certainty without sufficient IOCs/TTPs to support it?

5. **CrowdStrike Product Mapping** — Are suggested products actually relevant to the detected techniques?
   Are there products missing that would clearly apply?

6. **Platform Consistency** — Do techniques match stated target platforms?
   Windows-only techniques on Linux stages? Vice versa?

7. **Demonstrability Assessment** — Is demo_risk and demo_complexity rating realistic?
   Is the overall demonstrability conclusion supported by the attack stages?

- 75-89:  Good, a few issues to note but approved
- 60-74:  Acceptable, corrections recommended
- < 60:   Significant errors found, must be revised
